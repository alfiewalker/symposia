# Glossary

## Adjudication
The act of evaluating a claim or content and returning a verdict under explicit standards.

## Claim bundle
A structured set of subclaims extracted from the original input.

## Subclaim
A single adjudicable unit such as a factual statement, instruction, or inference.

## Juror
A single evaluator in the committee, usually backed by a model plus a role, prompt contract, and evidence view.

## Round 0
The independent first-pass adjudication round.

## Escalation
A bounded follow-up round triggered only when Round 0 is not decisive.

## Calibration
Empirical assessment of how well a juror or committee tracks known answers.

## Certainty
How strong the evidence and agreement are behind the verdict.

## Issuance
Whether the content is safe to issue or act on in its current form.

## Caveat
A qualification that materially changes how the content should be used or interpreted.

## Dissent
A structured record of material disagreement that survived aggregation.

## Trace
The auditable record of how the run reached its result.

## Profile
A named bundle of thresholds, policies, and execution rules.

## Domain policy
Rules specific to medicine, law, finance, or another domain that affect caution, escalation, and issuance.

## Validated
A public verdict meaning sufficiently supported to rely on under the current evidence and profile.

## Insufficient
A public verdict meaning there is not enough evidence or clarity to reach a supportable decision.

## Contested
A public verdict meaning there is real unresolved dispute after aggregation.
